WHAT EACH SCORE MEANS ▼
STRUCTUREZigZag swing structure (higher highs/lows pattern) combined with whether price is above or below the Value Area. The highest-priority layer — no trade is taken without structural alignment. Max: ±25 pts.
LOCATIONHow clean the entry location is relative to the Point of Control (POC), Value Area High (VAH), and Value Area Low (VAL). Trading at a defined level is higher quality than trading in open space. Max: ±15 pts.
MOMENTUMA combined vote from Trend Speed and MACD. Treated as one signal (both measure price rate-of-change from the same source). MACD modifies the Trend Speed base vote — it does not score independently. This eliminates double-counting. Max: ±15 pts.
ORDER FLOWCumulative Volume Delta (CVD) — whether real buy or sell volume is supporting the price direction. CVD divergence (price rising while buy volume declines) is a strong warning and applies a significant penalty. Max: ±20 pts.
VOLATILITYATR filter — never directional. Penalizes entries during dead markets (very low ATR) or when volatility has already spiked to extremes. A normal, expanding ATR environment applies no penalty. Max: −10 pts (filter only).

Base score starts at 50. All layers add or subtract. Final: ≥75 = Grade A, ≥60 = B, ≥50 = C, <50 = No Trade.

WHAT IS THE SYSTEM AUDIT? ▼

The System Audit runs on every signal and checks the structural quality of the decision — independent of whether the trade direction is correct. It asks: is this signal built on solid, independent logic, or are correlated indicators inflating each other?

PASSThis check passed cleanly. No structural issue in this dimension.
CAUTIONA potential issue exists but it is not blocking. The signal is usable but should be interpreted with awareness of the noted limitation.
FLAGGEDA structural problem was detected — for example, trade direction conflicts with regime, or a single indicator dominates the score. Does not cancel the signal, but demands extra judgment before acting.

The five dimensions checked:

INDEPENDENCEAre scoring layers measuring different market dimensions, or are some overlapping and inflating the final score?
BIAS CONTROLIs the signal free from self-reinforcing feedback loops where an indicator influences both regime classification and its own scoring?
SCORE VALIDITYIs the score built from multiple layers, or is one indicator dominating and masking weakness in the others?
REGIMEDoes the trade direction make sense given the current market regime? A long in EXHAUSTION is a warning. A long in REVERSAL is a serious concern.
STABILITYIs the confidence score near a grade boundary (e.g. 74 vs 75)? If so, small data changes could flip the grade — this is a fragility warning, not a disqualification.

Integrity Score: 45–50 = institutional grade. 35–44 = usable but fragile. Below 35 = apply significant skepticism.

WHAT IS TRADE INVALIDATION? ▼

Invalidation is the specific price action that tells you the original trade idea was wrong — not just temporarily against you, but structurally broken.

For a long trade: if the reason you entered was price holding above the Value Area High, then the trade is invalidated when price closes back below it. The setup no longer exists.

Knowing this level before you enter helps you define your maximum acceptable loss and maintain discipline. It is how the system avoids holding losing trades past the point where the edge has disappeared.
